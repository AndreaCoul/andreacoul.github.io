What to Pack

🏕️ Camping Gear

  • Sleeping bag (rated for cool nights)
  • Sleeping pad or foam mat
  • Flashlight or headlamp with extra batteries
  • Camping pillow
  • Tent stakes and guy lines (if needed)
  • Portable camping chair

👕 Clothing

  • 5-6 pairs of comfortable outdoor clothes
  • Warm layers (fleece jacket or sweater)
  • Waterproof or water-resistant jacket
  • 2-3 pairs of hiking boots/shoes (broken in!)
  • Socks (6-7 pairs, wool recommended)
  • Warm hat and sunhat
  • Gloves or mittens (nights can be cold)
  • Underwear and comfortable camp clothes
  • Pajamas
  • Swimsuit

🧴 Personal Hygiene

  • Biodegradable soap and shampoo
  • Toothbrush and toothpaste
  • Deodorant
  • Toilet paper and wet wipes
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Sunscreen (SPF 30+)
  • Insect repellent
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • Personal medications and first aid items
  • Feminine hygiene products (if needed)

🥗 Food & Beverages

  • Reusable water bottle (3+ liters)
  • Snacks (granola, trail mix, energy bars)
  • Dried fruits and nuts
  • Electrolyte drink mix
  • Any special dietary items
  • Insulated lunch container

📱 Electronics & Documents

  • Phone and charger (portable battery pack)
  • Camera (optional)
  • ID and insurance cards
  • Cash and credit card
  • Trip documents and confirmations
  • Headlamp batteries

🎒 Other Essentials

  • Backpack (30-40L capacity)
  • Daypack for hiking
  • Map and compass/GPS device
  • Multi-tool or knife
  • Rope or cordage
  • Duct tape (wrapped around a card)
  • Binoculars for bird watching
  • Notebook and pen for journaling
  • Card games or entertainment
  • Entertainment books or e-reader
  • Trash bag for Leave No Trace

💡 Packing Tips

  • Keep it light: Only pack what you truly need. The lighter your pack, the more enjoyable the hike.
  • Layer your clothing: Bring multiple layers so you can adjust to temperature changes throughout the day.
  • Wear broken-in shoes: Never wear brand new hiking boots on a camping trip!
  • Pack a headlamp: Essential for navigating the campsite at night.
  • Bring rain gear: Weather can be unpredictable in the mountains. Always be prepared!
  • Don't forget sunscreen: Even on cloudy days, UV rays can be strong at higher elevations.
  • Use packing cubes: They help organize your gear and make it easier to find items.
  • Waterproof your gear: Use dry bags or plastic liners to keep important items dry.
